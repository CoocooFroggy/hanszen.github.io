New Student Representative (Cabinet)

Type: Elected by all new students

Open to: New Students (including transfer students)

Number of positions: 3

Timeline: The ballot will be open from Tuesday at 9:00 PM to Friday at 9:00 PM, and the results will be announced shortly thereafter.

Description: As a New Student Represntative to Hanszen Cabinet, you will represent you class in our student government. Your responsibilities include voting in all cabinet decisions, making sure new students' voices are heard, organizing events for your class, and supplying food for cabinet once a month.


New Student Representative (Student Association)

Type: Appointed by Dani (Hanszen President) and Alison (Hanszen Senator)

Open to: New Students (including transfer students)

Number of positions: 2

Timeline: Apply using this form by Sunday, Sep 11 at 11:59 PM.

Description: As a New Student Represntative to the Student Association (Rice's campus-wide student government separate from all the college governments) you will represent both you class and Hanszen as a whole. Your responsibilities include making sure new students' and Hanszenites' voices are heard, as well as participating in various committees.

Civic Engagement Representative

Type: Appointed by the SA

Open to: All Hanszenites

Number of positions: 1

Timeline: Apply using this form by Friday, Sep 15 at 11:59 PM.

Description: The SA and RICEngaged are working collaboratively to create a cohort of Civic Engagement Reps at each residential college. Creating a college rep position will help increase civic engagement beyond what we’ve seen at Rice so far. College reps would be a resource that is available on campus, in the serveries, in college group chats—it’s more accessible than any other option currently available. College reps already have existing connections with other people in their college and it's these personal connections that will inspire people to go out and vote.

College reps would convene with a community of 10 other reps as part of the SA subcommittee on Democratic Engagement. They will support all of their residential college’s different voices. They should believe that every Rice student has a part to play in keeping our democracy healthy and want to promote voting and political awareness on campus. Lastly, they will empower Rice students to become active, engaged citizens capable of mobilizing their communities to create positive social change.
