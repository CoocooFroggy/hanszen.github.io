Facilities

Buildings Rooms Social & Entertainment Study Spaces

Old Section

Hanszen Old Section is so named because it is the oldest part of Hanszen. Built in 1916, the building predates even the founding of Hanszen College. Old Section contains a variety of rooms from singles to quads, but it has primarily doubles, with hall bathrooms. The first floor is also the home of Bryn and Neal Dugre, two of our Resident Associates. Both the second and third floor boast interior hallways where the majority of old section rooms can be found. If you keep going up to the fourth floor, you'll find an area called the Weenie Loft, which houses both a TV/movie room and a study room. Old section also has a five story tower with an attached sundeck.

Old Sid Richardson

For the 2021-2022 school year only, during the construction of New New Section, part of Hanszen lives in Sid Richardson College's old building. The tower is seven split-level stories, each with an upper and a lower floor. Hanszenites occupy the second, fifth, sixth and seventh floors of the building with the third and fourth floors reserved for other colleges. Nick Bourgeois and Lebena Varghese, two of our Resident Associates, live on the second floor, and our Resident Faculty Associates Lam and Rebecca Yu live on the fifth floor.

Commons

Hanszen Commons consists of two parts: lower commons and upper commons. Lower Commons has the entrance to South Servery, as well as a multitude of tables so that you can eat meals, catch up, debate whatever's on your mind, and/or joke with your fellow Hanszenites. It also includes Joyce's office, a kitchen, and a laundry room. It's here that Hanszenites will make announcements about events during meal times. Lower Commons even has a grand piano for your mealtime serenading desires, or for plinking out "Row Row Row Your Boat" late at night when you think no one's listening.

Upper Commons consists of a large open area with couches and numerous smaller rooms. You'll probably visit the open area at least once a week for Hanszen Cabinet meetings. Also in Upper Commons there is a game room with pool, foosball, and poker tables, several nice study rooms, a music room, a private dining room, and Hanszen's own library.

New New Section (Under Construction)

Called such to distinguish it from the old "New Section" built in 1957, New New Section will be Hanszen's newest building once it is completed in Summer 2022. New New Section is five stories tall with single and double rooms. Singles share a hall bathroom in groups of 2-3 rooms, and doubles have private bathrooms. The building will host a lobby, two RA suites, three terraces overlooking the quad and patio, and reading nooks on every floor. Built with sustainability in mind, New New Section uses a technique known as mass timber construction. It will be the first building in all of Houston of its kind. The building also incorporates the iconic framed crests and colored tiles from the exterior of the old New Section.

New Section (Recently Deceased)

The old "New Section" was built in 1957, the same year Hanszen College was founded. Composed entirely of suites, the building was known for its quirky architecture, spacious and fun rooms, and structural problems in its later years. The building hosted a landry room, a sundeck (called the moondeck to avoid confusion with the Old Section sundeck), and the legendary B&P. The B&P (Breadsticks and Pomegranites) was a legendary social space where all sorts of events would occur. Due to said problems, a lack of accessibility, and being an inefficient use of space, New Section was torn down in Summer 2021. Hanszenites have mixed feelings towards their former home. On one hand, the rooms layout was spacious and fun and the B&P was the cultural hub of Hanszen. On the other hand, the rooms and bathrooms were nasty, floods were common, and something just didn't seem right with the builidng.

All Hanszen rooms come with a twin XL sized bed, a desk and chair, a built-in or standing wardrobe, dressers, bookshelves, ethernet ports, trash cans, and TV cable jacks.

Old Section Double

The Old Section Double is what most people imagine when they think of a stereotypical “college room.” Depending on how much stuff the two people inside it have, doubles can range from spacious to cozy, but don’t worry: there is plenty of space for the creative mind.

Old Section Quad

An Old Section Quad is basically two doubles with a door connecting them. Some Hanszenites choose to put two beds in each room, while others put all four beds in the inner room and treat the outer room as a common space.

Old Sid Vator Suite

One of two types of suite in Old Sid, Vator Suites have two entrances on either side of the floor lobby. Each double is connected by the bathroom in the middle with two sinks, two toilets and a shower.

Old Sid Corner Suite

The other type of Old Sid suite, these rooms contain two doubles connected by a common bathroom with separate sink and toilet.

Other Rooms

There are also singles and triples in Old Section with various configurations.

New New Section Single

Ever wanted to have your own room? If so, the New New Section Single is for you. Highly sought after, singles are clustered in groups of 2-3 with a hall bathroom to share.

New New Section Double

While not as coveted as a single, the New New Section Double is an incredible room in its own right. A spacious room with a private bathroom and lots of storage space.

Hanszen is proud of our Commons Culture, which means we like to congregate in common spaces rather than stay in our rooms, and our spaces reflect this.

Game Room

You can find Hanszen's Game Room in the corner of our Upper Commons, complete with a TV, pool table, a foosball table, and couches. It even contains a brand-new poker table and a selection of card and board games for late-night fun with your friends.

Sundeck

Sundeck, located on the 5th floor of Old Section, offers a panoramic views of the campus and surrounding city and is the ideal spot for Friday evening parties or viewings of the sunset or sunrise.

Movie Room

As its name implies, the Movie Room (on the 4th floor of Old Section) contains several reclining couches and a flatscreen TV, great for movie nights with friends. It's also got an Xbox One complete with Rockband guitars, a drumset, and even a microphone, so be sure to stop by if you want to pretend to be musically talented.

Music Room

Just to the right of the Commons elevator, Hanszen's Music Room and its soundproofing and assorted instruments is the place to be for practicing the piano or rehearsing with your band and is a good practice room for music students.

Kitchen

Situated just a few steps from South Servery, Hanszen's kitchen has everything you need to bake late- night cookies with your friends or cook dinner on Saturday Nights.

PDR (Private Dining Room)

Perfect for a quiet study spot or a lunch meeting with your club, the Private Dining Room (PDR) can be reserved by any Hanszenite or organization that puts in a request to use it.

As much fun as we like to have at Hanszen, we acknowledge that the reason we are at Rice is to learn, so we have a plethora of studying spaces, here ordered from the most isolated to the most social.

Weenie Loft

Located in the 4th floor of Old Section, Hanszen’s eclectic attic has tables, couches, and chalk board walls that are sure to help you work hard.

Computer Room

Located in the Upper Commons, our computer room contains several free-to-use PC and Mac computers, as well as a black and white printer and a whiteboard.

Library

Also in the Upper Commons, Hanszen’s library has walls lined with books, ample natural lighting and even a chalk board.

Upper Commons

One of Hanszen's most recently improved spaces, the Upper Commons has several brand-new couches as well as tables that overlook the lower commons, so it's the perfect space to study, socialize, or take a midday nap. One of the walls is even painted to be a chalkboard!

Lower Commons

The first floor of Hanszen's Commons, also known as the Lower Commons, is always full of people doing problem sets together or cramming for exams, and is for sure our most social study environment, but also offers a small hangout area with a TV (great for Netflix study breaks!).
